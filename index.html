About Me

Hi, I'm Sinclaire!

I am currently a first-year graduate student in the Department of Astronomy at The Ohio State University. I completed my undergraduate degree in Astrophysics at Princeton University. I study the detection and characterization of exoplanets and their host stars.

I am also passionate about working to increase diversity and representation in the fields of Physics and Astronomy. In my free time, I enjoy spending time with my family, and I dabble in astrophotography.

My Work

My research primarily centers around TESS exoplanet candidate follow-up, as well as M-dwarf spectral characterization. Research Interests

Detection

HPF-TESS follow-up of M-dwarf systems with ground-based photometry and HPF radial velocity measurements

Stellar Characterization

Deriving spectral parameters using HPF-SpecMatch code. Identifying stellar activity via photometry analysis (i.e. stellar rotation, starspots, flaring)

Planetary Characterization

Transit photometry, radial velocity, Gaussian-process, and transit-timing variation modeling of planet candidates
